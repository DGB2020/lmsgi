About 82% of Americans live in urban areas (including suburbs);[222] about half of those reside in cities with populations over 50,000.[282] In 2008, 273 incorporated municipalities had populations over 100,000, nine cities had more than one million residents, and four global cities had over two million (New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, and Houston).[283]
The land area of the entire United States is approximately 3,800,000 square miles (9,841,955 km2),[218]
with the contiguous United States making up 2,959,064 square miles (7,663,940.6 km2) of that.
The United States is the world's third- or fourth-largest nation by total area (land and water), ranking behind Russia and Canada and just above or below China.
English (specifically, American English) is the de facto national language of the United States.
Although there is no official language at the federal level, some laws—such as U.S. naturalization requirements—standardize English
The First Amendment of the U.S. Constitution guarantees the free exercise of religion and forbids Congress from passing laws respecting its establishment. In a 2013 survey, 56% of Americans said religion played a "very important role in their lives", a far higher figure than that of any other Western nation.[302]
In a 2009 Gallup poll, 42% of Americans said they attended church weekly or almost weekly; the figures ranged from a low of 23% in Vermont to a high of 63% in Mississippi.[303]
